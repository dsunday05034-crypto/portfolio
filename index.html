Who I Am

DS

Economics graduate turned backend engineer with a passion for designing high-performance systems and solving complex structural problems.

I've always been fascinated by how complex systems work. Studying Economics gave me a deep appreciation for managing scarce resources and solving structural puzzles—but I wanted to actually build the solutions. Turning to software engineering felt completely natural; it allowed me to take that analytical mindset and apply it directly to writing clean, efficient code.

Right now, my technical world centers around Go (Golang). I love it because it forces you to write simple, reliable backend systems that perform exceptionally well under pressure. Alongside Go, I write clean JavaScript, HTML, and CSS to make sure whatever I build looks and feels great from the user's perspective, too.

Most of my growth comes from getting my hands dirty—diving into technical documentation, breaking things until I understand why they fail, and building real-world projects. I'm looking to step into a professional software engineering role where I can drop into a sharp team, pull my weight on meaningful backend infrastructure, and keep raising my engineering standards.
